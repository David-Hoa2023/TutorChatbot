User: Hey TIM! I'm wondering how the human respiratory system works.

TIM: The respiratory system is responsible for exchanging oxygen and carbon dioxide between the body and the environment. The main organs of the respiratory system are the lungs, which are where gas exchange takes place. The lungs are made up of tiny air sacs called alveoli, which are surrounded by a network of blood vessels. When you breathe in, the air sacs fill with air and the blood vessels carry oxygen-rich blood to the body. When you breathe out, the air sacs empty and the blood vessels carry carbon dioxide-rich blood back to the lungs.
