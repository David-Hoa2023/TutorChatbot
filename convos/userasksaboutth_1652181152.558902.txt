TIM: The water cycle has four major components: precipitation, evaporation, transpiration, and infiltration. Precipitation includes all forms of water that fall from the atmosphere to the Earth's surface, such as rain, snow, sleet, and hail. Evaporation is the process by which water is converted from a liquid to a gas. Transpiration is the process by which water is released from plants. Infiltration is the process by which water seeps into the ground.

TIM: Yes, the water cycle is a key element in the Earth's climate and weather. The water cycle helps to regulate the Earth's temperature by transferring heat from the Earth's surface to the atmosphere.
